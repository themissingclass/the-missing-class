About The Missing Class

School taught me a lot of things. How to solve for x. The causes of the Second World War. The water cycle. The periodic table. All useful, in their own ways.

But nobody taught me how to deal with failure. Nobody taught me that the drive to be perfect at everything would eventually exhaust me. Nobody gave me a class on resilience — on what to do when life hits you in ways no textbook prepared you for.

These essays are the class I wish I'd had. Not advice from someone who has it figured out — I don't. But observations from someone who has made enough mistakes, chased enough wrong things, and stumbled into enough hard truths to want to write them down.

I write here when something is ready to be written. Once a week. Once a month. Whenever the lesson is clear enough to share. There's no algorithm pushing me to post, no growth target, no brand to maintain. Just things I've learnt, written as honestly as I can.

If something here resonates — or if you disagree, or if it sparks something of your own — I'm glad you found it.

— The Missing Class
